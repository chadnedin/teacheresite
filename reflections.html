Monthly Reflections Timeline

Each month captures a major shift in my teaching practice, from hands-on curriculum design to capstone-level experiential learning.

October 2025

Developing Hands-On Tech Curriculum

Designing introductory Arduino and IoT projects highlighted the value of tactile learning.

Breaking down circuits and tilt sensors into interactive steps kept students engaged and helped build early confidence with hardware.

December 2025

Community STEM Leadership

Delivering the Service Smart Kids workshop with Big Brothers Big Sisters of Sarnia-Lambton was a major learning moment.

Teaching Python, cybersecurity, and robotics to youth reinforced my commitment to accessible education and pushed me to adapt instruction for diverse, non-traditional settings.

January 2026

Practicum and Assessment Strategies

Teaching Computer Studies, Math, and Tech (TAS1O) at St. Patrick's Catholic High School helped me bridge theory and practice.

Using tools like Logisim for digital logic and Boolean algebra showed the power of visual learning and sharpened my approach to structured assessment and technical feedback.

February 2026

Industry to Classroom Integration

Curating final artifacts made it clear how my programming and network engineering background shapes my pedagogy.

Connecting industry standards to classroom concepts, through Python projects and real-world examples, is now a cornerstone of my teaching philosophy.

March 2026

Expanding Teachable Horizons and Industry Alignment

After practicum, I focused on long-term growth and explored AQ/ABQ pathways in Communications Technology and Tech Design.

Balancing my leadership role at Syntexa Inc. with teaching reinforced a key belief: students benefit from exposure to modern, collaborative, problem-solving workflows.

April 2026

Digital Accessibility and Experiential Capstones

Finalizing this e-portfolio became an opportunity to practice what I teach.

Adding skip links, semantic HTML, and reduced-motion support reflects my commitment to accessibility and inclusive design. Facilitating RPAS workshops for Grade 8 and SHSM students also confirmed that authentic, hands-on challenges drive engagement and responsibility.
