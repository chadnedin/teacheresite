My Teaching Philosophy

Beliefs about Learning

I believe that learners construct knowledge through meaningful interactions with content, peers, and their environment. Students bring funds of knowledge that should be connected to curriculum goals. Learning is most effective when tasks are authentic, scaffolded, and accompanied by formative feedback.

Classroom Environment

I aim to build a respectful, predictable, and safe classroom where students feel valued and encouraged to take risks. Classroom routines, clear expectations, and restorative practices help create an environment conducive to academic and social-emotional growth.

Use of Technology in Education

Technology is a tool to enhance learning, personalize instruction, and provide students with opportunities to create and connect. I use tech purposefully — for collaboration, formative assessment, and to provide access to diverse resources.

Inclusivity and Equity

Every learner deserves equitable access to rigorous, culturally relevant instruction. I strive to differentiate learning, remove barriers, and reflect critically on my own practice to ensure inclusivity and respect for diverse identities and needs.

Reflection: How My Philosophy Has Evolved

During the program, my philosophy has shifted from a content-first focus to a learner-centred approach. I now place greater emphasis on assessment for learning and relationship-building as central to effective teaching.
